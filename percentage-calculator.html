How to Calculate Percentages — Complete Guide

Percentages are one of the most commonly used mathematical concepts in everyday life. Whether you're calculating a discount while shopping, determining a tip at a restaurant, analyzing business growth, or working on academic assignments, understanding how to calculate percentages is essential. Our free online percentage calculator makes these computations instant and accurate.

Basic Percentage Formula

The fundamental formula to find a percentage of a number is straightforward:

Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100

For example, if you want to find 25% of 200: (25 ÷ 100) × 200 = 50. Our "What is X% of Y?" calculator above performs this calculation instantly.

Percentage Increase & Decrease

Percentage change calculations are crucial for understanding growth or decline in values over time. This is widely used in:

  • Finance & Investing — Tracking stock price changes, portfolio returns, and revenue growth
  • Business Analytics — Measuring sales growth, customer acquisition rates, and market share changes
  • Economics — Calculating inflation rates, GDP growth, and employment statistics
  • Everyday Life — Understanding price changes, salary raises, and utility bill fluctuations
Percentage Change = ((New Value - Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100

A positive result indicates an increase, while a negative result indicates a decrease. For instance, if a product's price goes from $80 to $100, the percentage increase is ((100 - 80) ÷ 80) × 100 = 25%.

Percentage Difference vs. Percentage Change

These two concepts are often confused but serve different purposes. Percentage change compares a new value to an old value (directional), while percentage difference compares two values without implying which is the reference (non-directional).

Percentage Difference = |Value₁ - Value₂| ÷ ((Value₁ + Value₂) ÷ 2) × 100

Use percentage difference when comparing two independent values, such as the heights of two people or the prices of two different products.
